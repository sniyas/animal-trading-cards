Orca

Orcas are often called killer whales. However, orcas are not actually whales; they are dolphins (and the largest species of dolphin, at that).

  • Scientific Name: Orcinus orca
  • Average Length: 16ft to 22ft
  • Average Lifespan: 30-50 years
  • Habitat: Oceans

The orca or killer whale is a toothed whale belonging to the oceanic dolphin family, of which it is the largest member. It is recognizable by its black-and-white patterned body. Orcas can be found in all of the world's oceans in a variety of marine environments, from Arctic and Antarctic regions to tropical seas.
